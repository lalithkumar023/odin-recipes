Recipe for a perfectly cripsy Dosai

Making a crispy dosa involves a few key steps and tips to get it just right. Here's a basic recipe and some tips to achieve crispy dosas:

Ingredients

  • cup rice (any variety, but typically a short-grain rice like sona masuri or idli rice)
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional, for flavor)
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking)

Steps

  1. Preparation:

    Wash the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds thoroughly under running water.

    Soak them together in enough water for about 6-8 hours or overnight. The soaking helps in easy grinding and fermentation.

  2. Grinding:

    After soaking, drain the water and grind the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds into a smooth batter. Add water as needed to get a smooth, slightly thick consistency.

    The batter should not be too runny.

  3. Fermentation:

    Cover the bowl loosely and let it ferment in a warm place for about 8-12 hours or until it doubles in volume.

    The fermentation process is crucial for the dosas to turn out crispy.

  4. Making Dosai:

    Heat a non-stick or cast iron dosa tawa (griddle) over medium-high heat.

    Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the hot tawa. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin, even layer.

    You can make it as thin as possible for crispier dosas.

  5. Folding:

    Using a flat spatula, gently loosen the edges of the dosa. Once it's crisp and golden brown, carefully fold it in half or roll it into a cylinder.
