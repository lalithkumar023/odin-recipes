Tasty Biriyani

Recipe for Tasty Biriyani

Here is the recipe for a flavorful, aromatic, and perfect single pot biriyani

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 250g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon biryani masala powder (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • A small handful of fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • A small handful of fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1.5 cups water

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken:

    In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, half of the biryani masala powder, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, half of the chopped coriander leaves, half of the chopped mint leaves, and salt.

    Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Sauté Onions and Spices:

    Heat ghee or oil in a deep, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick) and sauté for a minute until fragrant.

    Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

  3. Cook the Chicken:

    Add the marinated chicken along with the marinade to the pot. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the chicken starts to brown slightly.

  4. Layer and Cook the Rice:

    Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Gently mix everything together to coat the rice with the chicken and spices.

    Add the chopped tomato, green chili, and remaining biryani masala powder. Mix well.

    Pour water over the mixture. Check for salt and adjust seasoning if needed.

  5. Simmer and Dum Cooking:

    Bring the mixture to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes. Avoid stirring during this time to prevent the rice from breaking.

  6. Check Doneness:

    After 15-20 minutes, turn off the heat and let the biryani sit, covered, for another 5-10 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld together and the residual heat to finish cooking the rice.

  7. Serve:

    Serve hot as is or with raita (yogurt dip) or salad on the side.
